First, the core game loop. 

Stomp is a turn based game 2 player game. It borrows heavy inspiration from Pokemon. Each turn, you choose a move, your opponent chooses a move, and then they resolve. You might do some damage, you might heal some damage, you might change which monster you have out.

You can play a full game across a variety of CPU difficulties, with basically zero perceived latency. The game engine is transpiled to TypeScript (from Solidity!!!) so you get instant feedback. There's a rich and varied set of mechanics, and it all costs less than $0.01 in ETH! (Around 2-5 million gas for the nerds.)

If you've played JRPGs or turn-based games, a lot of the mechanics may be familiar to you. There's a resource system, various types of damage (damage over time, delayed damage, elemental charts), and also a few unique ideas (some mons can resurrect, others can target any mon, etc. etc.)
